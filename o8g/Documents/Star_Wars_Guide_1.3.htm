Star Wars: The Card Game

Setup

1.Choose your Force Allegiance and related faction. Take the three Force cards for your Allegiance.

2.Create token pool.

3.Set the Death Star Dial to 0.

4.Set the Balance of the Force to the Light Side.

5.Separate the Objective Cards from the Command Deck and shuffle both decks.

6.Draw 4 cards from the objective deck and choose 3. Place the 4th card facedown on the bottom of the objective deck.

7.Draw 6 cards from the command deck for the starting hand. Players may mulligan once.

8.Starting with the Dark Side player, reveal the 3 chosen objectives one at a time. Resolve any effects on the card before putting the next into play.

The game starts with the Dark Side player as the active player. Players then alternate after going through all 6 phases.

Game Phases

1. Balance Phase

If the DS player is active advance the Death Star dial by one point. If the Balance of the Force is with the dark side advance the Death Star dial one additional point.

The game ends immediately if the Death Star dial reaches or exceeds 12.

If the LS player is active, and the Balance of the Force is with the light side, the LS player may deal one damage to any one current DS objective.

2.Refresh Phase

1.The active player removes one focus token from each card in their play area.

On the LS player’s first turn they must skip this step of the Refresh Phase.

2.Remove all shield tokens from each card in the active player’s play area.

3.If the active player has fewer than 3 current objective cards replace each missing objective card by drawing the top card of the objective deck. Resolve any ‘When this card enters play’ effects.

3.Draw Phase

The active player may discard one card from their hand. Then draw/discard cards back to your current reserve value (default 6).

V1.3

4. Deployment Phase

The active player pays the resource costs to bring unit and enhancement cards from hand into play. They may play as many cards as desired as long as they are able to afford their costs. Place the appropriate number of focus tokens on resources as they are used to pay for cards.

Play unit cards faceup into their owner’s play area.

Play enhancement cards as appropriate (play area, an objective card, an affiliation, etc)

When paying resource costs at least one of the resource providing cards used must match the affiliation of the card being played from hand. Place the appropriate amount of focus tokens on the resource cards being used to pay for the card being played. Generated resources cannot be used to pay for multiple cards.

The cost of playing a neutral unit, enhancement, or event card does not require a resource match. Also neutral resource providing cards do not qualify as a resource match for any affiliation.

5. Conflict Phase

The active player may engage one or more enemy objective cards. Each current enemy objective card may only be engaged once per turn. Finish resolving an engagement before engaging a new objective.

On the DS player’s first turn they must skip this phase.

Resolving an Engagement

1.Declare Objective

•The active player declares which one enemy objective they will engage.

2.Declare Attackers

•The active player declares which of their ready units will engage. At least one unit must be declared as an attacker.

3.Declare Defenders

•The defending player declares which of his ready units will engage. Defending player has the option of declaring no defenders.

4.Fight Edge Battle

•Starting with the attacker place Edge Cards facedown one at a time. Then defender may place one Edge card facedown. Alternate until both players consecutively pass.

If no defenders have been declared the defending player cannot play edge cards and the attacker automatically wins the edge.

If no defenders have been declared the attacking player can still play edge cards. If the attacker does play edge cards continue the edge battle as normal.

•Both players reveal edge stacks simultaneously.

•Resolve fate cards in ascending order according to priority number.

•Finish the edge battle by counting the number of Force icons on the cards in each edge stack. The player with the higher total wins the edge battle and has the edge for the remainder of the engagement.

If the totals are tied the defending player has the edge.

5.Resolve Strikes

V1.3

•Starting with the player who has the edge, each player in turn focuses one of their participating ready units to strike. (If the unit is committed to the Force place one additional focus token on unit when focused to strike) Fully resolve each strike before the next may begin.

Shield tokens present on struck unit may be discarded to prevent one strike or one focus token being placed.

Destroyed units are placed faceup on top of its owner’s discard pile. Any attached cards are placed in their respective owner’s discard piles.

When a LS objective is placed in the DS victory pile advance the Death Star dial one point for each LS objective card in the DS victory pile.

When a DS objective card is destroyed any attached captured cards are considered rescued and returned to its owner’s hand.

6.Reward Unopposed

•If at least one attacking unit has survived, and there are no surviving defenders, deal one bonus damage to the engaged objective card.

6.Force Phase

The active player may commit any unit cards in their play area to the Force, up to the max limit of 3. (Except for a unit already committed to the force)

Each player adds the number of Force icons on their ready units that are committed to the Force. The player with the highest Force total flips the Balance of the Force to their side.

Winning the Game

Light Side Victory:

•3 or more DS objective cards are destroyed

•The DS player must draw a card from their command or objective deck but that deck is empty.

Dark Side Victory

•The Death Star dial advances to 12

•The LS player must draw a card from their command or objective deck but that deck is empty.

Custom Deck building

When building a custom deck it must contain a minimum of 10 objective cards. A deck must be built for either LS or DS play. A deck cannot use an affiliation card or include objective sets belonging to the other side of the force.

Any objective set may be included twice in a custom deck unless its objective card states “limit one per objective deck”

The affiliation of an objective set does not need to match the affiliation of a player’s affiliation card, unless the objective card includes “(name of) affiliation only”

Keywords

Edge [X]: The “Edge” keyword is always followed by a value. This value is the number of additional Force icons the card’s controller counts in an edge battle resolved during an engagement in which the card with the “Edge” keyword participates.

Elite: A card with the “Elite” keyword removes one additional focus token from itself (if able) when it refreshes during the refresh phase.

Limited: A player can play only one card with the “Limited” keyword during each turn.

No Enhancements: A card with the “No Enhancements” keyword cannot have enhancement cards attached to it.

Protect [Trait]: A card with the “Protect” keyword may take damage instead of any friendly card in play with the specified trait.

Shielding: When a card with the “Shielding” keyword is declared as either attacker or defender, its controller may immediately place one shield token on any friendly unshielded participating unit, or to the engaged objective card, if unshielded.

Targeted Strike: When a card with the “Targeted Strike” keyword strikes during an engagement as an attacker, damage from its unit damage icon type may be assigned to one target enemy unit card, even if that unit is not participating in the engagement.

Support (X): This card may be played as an enhancement to a friendly objective. In order to play the card as a support, the cost of X must be paid. The paranthetical text following "Support (X)" expresses the ability of the card while it is being used as a Support enhancement. A card being used as a Support enhancement retains any other text (outside the paranthetical support text) it may possess.
